Business tracking is a [framework](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2163970267/framework) that collects and provides event data for defined events to third party applications ([web analytics](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2177466691/web+analytics) engines and [recommendation engine](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2153813294/recommendation+engine)s, which collect data, generate reports and present the results). The business tracking framework defines events that can be triggered, provides [component](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2160842541/component)s to developers, and offers interfaces and managers for web analytics engines or recommendation engines respectively.

In management, business value is an informal term that includes all forms of value that determine the health and well-being of the firm. Business value expands concept of value of the firm beyond economic value to include other forms of value such as customer value, supplier value, partner value, and managerial value. Many of these forms of value are not directly measured in monetary terms.

Business value often embraces intangible assets not necessarily attributable to any stakeholder group. Examples include intellectual capital and a firm's business model.
