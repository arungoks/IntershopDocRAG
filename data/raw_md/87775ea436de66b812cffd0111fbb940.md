[feature](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2152516365/feature) | A feature is a defined piece of functionality (business or technical) delivered by a [product](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2160821932/product), a [solution](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2160844223/solution) or an [extension](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2160844228/extension). A feature is defined by one or more [system use case](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2152516473/system+use+case)s. |
[feature freeze](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2179414692/feature+freeze) | Feature freeze is a point in time in the development process after which all work on adding new [feature](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2152516365/feature)s is not allowed and the effort will shift towards fixing [defect](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2171864885/defect)s. |
[feature list](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2163975014/feature+list) | The feature list represents the developed [feature](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2152516365/feature)s over time and releases in the context of a [product](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2160821932/product) and/or [solution](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2160844223/solution) and/or [extension](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2160844228/extension). Feature lists serve two audiences: the internal audience to communicate all details to Intershop and the alliance, the external to communicate all necessary details to the market and customers. The owner is product management. |
[feature set](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2152516370/feature+set) | A group of [feature](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2152516365/feature)s that target a specific need. |
[feature wish](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2152516380/feature+wish) | A feature wish is a functional or non-functional [requirement](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2159969491/requirement) for a specific [feature](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2152516365/feature) requested by a customer or [stakeholder](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2159968484/stakeholder) phrased in a natural language. Feature wishes do not belong to a specific [product](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2160821932/product), [solution](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2160844223/solution) or [extension](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2160844228/extension). At Intershop, they are managed in Engineering JIRA projects. |

[framework](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2163970267/framework) |
Generally, a software framework provides the possibility to extend a software with new [feature](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2152516365/feature)s with the minimum possible development effort. From a developer's perspective, a framework is code intended to be specialized and/or instantiated in order to implement specific [feature](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2152516365/feature)s. |
