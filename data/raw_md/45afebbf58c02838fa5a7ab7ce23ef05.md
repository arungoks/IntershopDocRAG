A recommendation is a product or a service that fits a selected product. It is offered together with the selected one (e.g., cross selling, up selling). In [Intershop Commerce Management](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2217982072/Intershop+Commerce+Management), recommendations are known as [product link](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2160821953/product+link)s.

There are different kind of recommendations possible:

Dynamic recommendations can be created by a [recommendation engine](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2153813294/recommendation+engine) provided by a business partner.

A recommendation engine is a system (software) that generates [recommendation](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2153813283/recommendation)s based on provided information or business reports, for example, "[customer](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2153810357/customer)s who have bought this product have also bought these products".

A recommendation engine can be provided by an external business partner (3rd party) and integrated with [Intershop Commerce Management](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2217982072/Intershop+Commerce+Management) via the [managed service framework](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2160834517/managed+service+framework).

Based on an action by an actor or a system (e.g., "add to basket"), a recommendation event is the piece of information sent to a [recommendation engine](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2153813294/recommendation+engine) for it to "learn" about [customer](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2153810357/customer) behavior and preferences.

As opposed to the [recommendation request](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2178023772/recommendation+request), the recommendation event *feeds* information to the recommendation engine.

A recommendation request is a trigger for retrieving a product [recommendation](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2153813283/recommendation) from a [recommendation engine](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2153813294/recommendation+engine), usually sent upon a specific action by an actor or a system (e.g., viewing a product's details).

As opposed to the [recommendation event](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2178023769/recommendation+event), the recommendation request *asks* for information from the recommendation engine.

A recurring order refers to a regular purchase that happens at a set frequency (e.g., weekly, monthly) and typically requires manual intervention by the customer.

Customers may need to confirm or reorder at these intervals by adding items to their cart or authorizing payment. While a company might automatically generate the order based on the set schedule, the customer retains control over each transaction.

Example: Setting up a recurring order for monthly coffee delivery requires the customer to approve or pay for each subsequent order as it becomes due.

This concept is often compared to subscriptions, however, there are differences in terms of automation, customer involvement, and payment processes, see [subscription](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2227919888/subscription) for details.
