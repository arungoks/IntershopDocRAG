The Object Path Expression Language (also object path) is an Intershop expression language that provides dynamic access to properties of objects and several basic functions and operators. Object Path expressions can be used in [Intershop Markup Language](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2167931481/Intershop+Markup+Language) [ISML template](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2160822230/ISML+template)s, queries and [pipeline](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2160821914/pipeline)s.

From a developer's perspective, object replication describes the mechanism for transferring individual [business object](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2158101810/business+object)s from a [source cluster](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2160822129/source+cluster) (or "editing system") to a [target cluster](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2160822228/target+cluster) (typically, a "live system" or "production system"). As opposed to the [data replication](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2155352933/data+replication), which transfers large amounts of data usually at regular intervals, object replication is intended to transfer individual (or a limited number of) objects in an ad-hoc fashion.

OCI punchout is an [Open Catalog Interface](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Open_Catalog_Interface)-based mechanism by which a [business customer](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2180154362/business+customer)'s purchasing system makes it possible for a [buyer](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2153817105/buyer) to access a [merchant](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2158755994/merchant)'s [web shop](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2179410721/web+shop) from the purchasing system. The buyer leaves ("punches out") the purchasing system and enters the merchant's web shop within the buyer’s browser frame. The buyer browses the web shop and adds items to the [shopping cart](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2160822116/shopping+cart) while both applications maintain their connections. No orders are sent to the merchant when the user checks out from the merchant’s punchout site. Instead, the shopping cart with the selected items is brought back to the purchasing system, which proceeds through the established workflow steps.

"Omni-channel commerce" is a commerce paradigm that is characterized by the integration of all commerce channels and their respective touchpoints (e.g. retail store, call center, mobile, online store, POS). It enables customers to experience a seamless shopping experience while using multiple channels simultaneously.

For customers, it is especially the parallel usage of several channels at the same time, that differentiates omni-channel from multi-channel. On the merchant’s side, the technical challenge in fulfilling this scenario is the seamless and real time data integration and data access, which is based on the centralized view of all data. Centrally managing all data, such as product data, transactional data, customer data, order data and inventory data is a precondition in this context.

Omni-channel commerce focuses on understanding the customer’s patterns and preferences and on truly supporting the customer’s specific needs. This is only possible by optimizing people, processes and technology to serve customers across all touchpoints.

The understanding of "channel" as a "commerce channel" is different from the Intershop-internal use of [channel](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2190215052/channel) in our software.

Intershop's Omni-Channel Services Toolset is a collection of [framework](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2163970267/framework)s that enable fast, standardized development of business web [app](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2152517685/application)s like the [Intershop Contact Center](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2195271048/Intershop+Contact+Center). Based on open-source technologies, the OCST is decoupled from the [Intershop Commerce Management](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2217982072/Intershop+Commerce+Management) core platform and uses the [REST API](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2214729930/REST+framework) (as part of the [Synaptic Commerce API](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2214745183/Synaptic+Commerce+API)) to access data and commerce functionality.

There is a payment method called "open invoice". The consumer pays the invoice via bank transfer when the delivery has been received and accepted by the consumer.

From the consumer's point of view the payment method "open invoice" is a secure payment method since he/she receives the ordered products and has to pay the invoice after the delivery.

From the client's point of view it is an insecure payment method, since the products will be delivered when the related order / invoice has not been paid yet. That is why the client should consider under which preconditions this payment method will be offered.

In business or commerce, an order is a stated intention, either spoken or written, to engage in a commercial transaction for specific products or services.

When the purchase order of the customer and the sales order of the merchant agree, the orders become a contract between the customer and merchant.

The contract between the customer and the merchant comes into force when the products have been delivered and the delivery has been accepted by the customer. The contract covers the delivered products only.

These conditions have to be provided in the [general terms and conditions](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2153811847/general+terms+and+conditions).

A cancellation does not mean that all related order information have to be deleted in the involved systems. The order information should be kept to track the history.
