[UCM](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2190216272/UCM) | UCM ("Unified Commerce Management") was announced by Intershop as a "best-practices strategy for managing commerce processes and models across the extended enterprise". The idea was that a UCM-based system enabled companies to manage commerce relationships between their customers, partners, and suppliers, creating an "outside-in" perspective and finally resulting in a customer-driven commerce environment. |
[unfair business practice](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2153813358/unfair+business+practice) | Unfair business practices encompass fraud, misrepresentation, and oppressive or unconscionable acts or practices by businesses, often against [customer-x]s and are prohibited by law in many countries. For instance, in the European Union, each member state must regulate unfair business practices in accordance with the Unfair Commercial Practices Directive, subject to transitional periods. Unfair business practices may arise in many areas, including: - tenancy matters
- matters involving the purchase of products and services by consumers
- matters involving insurance claims and the settlement thereof
- debt collection in cases of default

[update](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2180167660/update) | In computing, an update is a piece of software designed to fix problems and/or to improve the usability or performance. At Intershop, update means applying a [patch](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2177466681/patch) or [hotfix](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2177466685/hotfix) to an existing [Intershop Commerce Management](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2217982072/Intershop+Commerce+Management) version. As opposed to an [upgrade](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2180167664/upgrade), the installed software instance is not replaced. |
[upgrade](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2180167664/upgrade) | In computing, upgrade refers to the replacement of a product (hardware, software) with a newer or better version of the same product in order to bring the system up to date or to improve its characteristics. At Intershop, upgrade means replacing an [Intershop Commerce Management](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2217982072/Intershop+Commerce+Management) version with a higher major or minor [version](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2171864888/version) (see [migration](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2180161414/migration)). As opposed to an [update](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2180167660/update), the originally installed software instance is not kept. |

[user story](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2163976840/user+story) | A user story is a simple statement about what a user wants to do with a [feature](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2152516365/feature) of the software, written from the user’s perspective. In agile software development, user stories are used to capture the [requirement](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2159969491/requirement)s throughout a [project](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2159968496/project). |

[UX](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2165377951/UX) | A person's perceptions and responses that result from the use or anticipated use of a product, system or service (ISO 9241-210). That is, user experience involves a person's behaviors, attitudes, and emotions about using a particular system, including aspects such as utility, ease of use and efficiency, which generally comprises [IAD](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2163976934/IAD) and [VD](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2163976958/VD). |
