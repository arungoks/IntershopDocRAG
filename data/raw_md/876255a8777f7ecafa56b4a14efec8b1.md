Platform as a service (PaaS) is a facet of [cloud computing](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2230485152/cloud+computing). It involves services that provides a platform allowing customers to develop, run, and manage [application](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2152517685/application)s without the complexity of building and maintaining the infrastructure typically associated with developing and launching an application.

The capability provided to the customer is to deploy onto the cloud infrastructure customer-created or acquired applications created using programming languages, libraries, services, and tools supported by the provider. The customer does not manage or control the underlying cloud infrastructure (network, servers, operating systems, or storage), but has control over the deployed applications and possibly configuration settings for the application-hosting environment.

A payment service provider (PSP) is a company that offers [merchant](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2158755994/merchant)s online services for accepting electronic payments by a variety of [payment method](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2153813230/payment+method)s including [credit card](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2153810369/credit+card), bank-based payments such as [direct debit](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2153810439/direct+debit), bank transfer, and real-time bank transfer based on online banking.

Typically, a PSP can connect to multiple acquiring banks, card and payment networks. A personalization group is a temporary group of users ([customer](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2153810357/customer)s) for which the [storefront](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2178600227/storefront) must be personalized in the same way. The [personalization](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2177466606/personalization) is done for groups of users rather than for single users for efficiency reasons. This reduces the number of possible page variations and allows for better [page caching](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2160821816/page+cache) and reuse of personalized pages.

The personalization group membership for a user can be determined using various criteria, like authentication state (anonymous, logged in), registration state, [customer segment](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2172519751/customer+segment), [address](https://knowledge.intershop.com/kb/go.php/a/ISGLOS/pages/2152519075/address) etc. The personalization group membership may change with the users interaction in the storefront, like after authentication (login, logout), registration at the store or other actions.

Personalization groups are expressed by a PGID, which is contained in the URL. The PGID is important to distinct between personalized pages with otherwise equal URLs. For example, the same product page can have multiple instances with different prices, one for each personalization group.
